Understanding Knee Osteoarthritis

Osteoarthritis is the most common form of arthritis, affecting millions of people worldwide. It occurs when the protective cartilage that cushions the ends of your bones wears down over time.

Common Symptoms
  • Pain during or after movement
  • Joint stiffness upon waking up
  • Loss of joint flexibility
  • Grating sensation or "popping"
  • Bone spurs around the joint
Primary Causes
  • Old age and natural wear/tear
  • Previous joint injuries or tears
  • Repeated joint stress
  • Genetics and bone deformities
  • Certain metabolic diseases
Prevention Methods
  • Maintain a healthy body weight
  • Control blood sugar levels
  • Be active but protect your joints
  • Avoid repetitive high-stress movements
  • Rest when joints feel painful
Doctor's Advice
  • Consult if pain lasts > 3 days
  • Engage in low-impact activities like swimming
  • Use supportive braces if needed
  • Follow prescribed physiotherapy
